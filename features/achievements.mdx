Achievements are special badges you earn by reaching specific coding milestones. Each achievement awards Dump Points and showcases your accomplishments on your public profile.

## How Achievements Work

Achievements unlock automatically when you meet their criteria. You don't need to claim them manually - the system checks your activity continuously and awards achievements as you qualify.

**Achievement components:**
- **Icon** - Visual badge representing the achievement
- **Title** - Achievement name (e.g., "Early Bird")
- **Description** - How to earn it
- **Points** - Dump Points awarded when unlocked
- **Criteria Type** - Category (streak, time, language, etc.)

<Info>
Once earned, achievements remain on your profile permanently. They cannot be lost, even if your stats change later.
</Info>

## Available Achievements

CoreDump currently offers 10 achievements across different categories:

### First Activity
**How to earn:** Record your very first coding activity
- Points: 10
- Category: Time-based
- Difficulty: Automatic

This achievement unlocks the moment you log your first coding session with the CoreDump editor plugin. Everyone earns this achievement.

<Tip>
This is your welcome achievement! It confirms your editor plugin is working correctly.
</Tip>

### 7-Day Streak
**How to earn:** Log coding activity for 7 consecutive days
- Points: 50
- Category: Streak
- Difficulty: Easy

Code every day for a full week without skipping a single day. Even a few minutes of coding each day counts.

### 30-Day Streak
**How to earn:** Keep your streak alive for 30 consecutive days
- Points: 250
- Category: Streak
- Difficulty: Medium

Maintain a full month of daily coding. This achievement demonstrates serious commitment and consistency.

<Note>
Streak achievements require consecutive days. If your streak breaks at day 29, you need to rebuild from day 1.
</Note>

### Language Explorer
**How to earn:** Record activity in 5 different programming languages
- Points: 75
- Category: Language count
- Difficulty: Easy

Use at least five unique programming languages in your projects. Languages count when you have any tracked time in them.

**Examples of qualifying languages:**
- JavaScript, Python, Go, Rust, Java
- TypeScript, C++, Ruby, PHP, Swift
- Any language the CoreDump plugin recognizes

### Polyglot
**How to earn:** Record activity in 10 different programming languages
- Points: 200
- Category: Language count
- Difficulty: Medium

Expand your language usage to 10 unique languages. This shows versatility across different tech stacks and project types.

<Tip>
Full-stack developers and those working on diverse projects earn this achievement naturally. Try new languages in side projects to accelerate progress.
</Tip>

### Marathon Session
**How to earn:** Single coding session of 4 hours or more
- Points: 100
- Category: Session time
- Difficulty: Medium

Log at least 4 continuous hours of coding activity in one session. The session ends when you close your editor or stop coding for an extended period.

<Info>
This measures total duration for one language, not continuous active typing. Natural breaks are fine as long as you stay in the editor.
</Info>

### Early Bird
**How to earn:** Log activity between 5:00-6:00 AM local time
- Points: 20
- Category: Time of day
- Difficulty: Easy (if you wake early)

Code during the early morning hour from 5:00 AM to 5:59 AM. The system uses the timestamp of your coding activity.

**Tips for early birds:**
- Set up your coding environment the night before
- Start a morning coding routine before work
- Tackle challenging problems when your mind is fresh

### Night Owl
**How to earn:** Log activity between 2:00-4:00 AM local time
- Points: 20
- Category: Time of day
- Difficulty: Easy (if you code late)

Code during the late-night window from 2:00 AM to 3:59 AM. Perfect for night owls and developers in flow states.

<Tip>
You can earn both Early Bird and Night Owl if you code at different times. They're not mutually exclusive.
</Tip>

### Century Coder
**How to earn:** Accumulate 100 hours of recorded coding time
- Points: 300
- Category: Cumulative time
- Difficulty: Hard

Reach 100 total hours of coding activity across all languages and projects. This achievement recognizes significant time investment in your craft.

**Progress tracking:**
- Check your "Total Time" stat on your dashboard
- 100 hours = 6,000 minutes of tracked coding
- At 2 hours/day, this takes about 50 days

### Daily Marathon
**How to earn:** Have a single day with 8 or more hours of coding
- Points: 120
- Category: Daily total
- Difficulty: Medium-Hard

Log at least 8 hours of coding activity within a single calendar day (midnight to midnight UTC).

<Note>
This is harder than Marathon Session because it's measured by calendar day, not continuous session. You could have multiple sessions totaling 8+ hours.
</Note>

## Achievement Categories

Achievements are organized into five types:

### Streak-Based
Reward consistent daily coding:
- 7-Day Streak

Reward language diversity:
- Language Explorer (5 languages)

### Time-Based

### Daily Total
Reward high-volume days:
- Daily Marathon (8 hours in one day)

<Info>

</Info>

## Dump Points System

Each achievement awards Dump Points when unlocked:

**Point values:**
- First Activity: 10 points
- Early Bird: 20 points
- Night Owl: 20 points
- 7-Day Streak: 50 points
- Language Explorer: 75 points
- Marathon Session: 100 points
- Daily Marathon: 120 points
- Polyglot: 200 points
- 30-Day Streak: 250 points
- Century Coder: 300 points

**Maximum achievement points:** 1,145 points (if all 10 achievements unlocked)

<Tip>
Dump Points from achievements contribute to your total points, which affect your leaderboard ranking. Strategic achievement hunting can boost your position.
</Tip>

## Tracking Achievement Progress

You can track your achievement progress in several ways:

### Dashboard View
Your dashboard shows:
- "Achievements: X/10" in the stats grid
- Full achievement gallery below language breakdown
- Visual indicators for locked vs unlocked

### Profile View
Your public profile displays:
- All unlocked achievements prominently
- Achievement count in statistics
- Visual badge gallery

### Progress Indicators
For achievements with measurable progress:
- **Streaks:** Check current streak stat
- **Languages:** Count unique languages in language breakdown
- **Total time:** View "Total Time" stat
- **Today's time:** Check if approaching 8 hours for Daily Marathon

<Note>
There's no separate "achievement progress" page currently. Monitor your stats to estimate how close you are to unlocking each achievement.
</Note>

## Achievement Display

Unlocked achievements appear on your profile with:
- Full-color icon/badge
- Achievement name
- Description of criteria
- Points awarded

Locked achievements may appear:
- Grayed out or hidden depending on view
- With description showing how to unlock
- As mystery achievements (name hidden until earned)

<Info>
Other developers can see all achievements you've unlocked when viewing your public profile.
</Info>

## Common Achievement Questions

**Q: Can I lose an achievement after earning it?**
A: No. Achievements are permanent once unlocked.

**Q: Do achievements unlock retroactively?**
A: Yes. When you first join CoreDump, existing activity may unlock achievements immediately.

**Q: Can I earn the same achievement twice?**
A: No. Each achievement unlocks once per account.

**Q: What if I had a 30-day streak that broke? Do I keep the achievement?**
A: Yes. The achievement is permanent even if your current streak is lower.

**Q: Are more achievements coming?**
A: Yes. CoreDump regularly adds new achievements and challenges.

<Info>
Watch for updates to the achievement system. New badges, point values, and categories are added based on community feedback.
</Info>
