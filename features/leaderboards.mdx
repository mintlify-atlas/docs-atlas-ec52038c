The CoreDump leaderboard system lets you compete with developers around the world based on coding activity. Track your ranking, see who's leading, and push yourself to code more consistently.

## How Leaderboards Work

The leaderboard ranks all CoreDump users based on their coding time within a specific time period. Your position is determined by the total minutes of coding activity you've logged compared to other developers.

### Two Time Ranges

CoreDump offers two leaderboard views:

**Today (24 Hours)**
Ranks developers by coding time in the last 24 hours. This leaderboard resets at midnight UTC each day, giving everyone a fresh start.

**This Week (7 Days)**
Ranks developers by total coding time over the last 7 days. This leaderboard resets every Monday at midnight UTC.

<Info>
You can switch between "Today" and "This Week" views using the toggle buttons at the top of the leaderboard page.
</Info>

## Understanding Rankings

Each leaderboard entry shows:

- **Rank position** (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.)
- **Developer name** and profile image
- **Total coding time** for the selected period
- **Current streak** of consecutive coding days
- **Top language** used during the period

### Special Positions

The top performer receives special recognition:
- Featured at the top of the leaderboard page
- Highlighted with distinctive styling
- Shown as "Leaderboard Topper"

<Tip>
Click on any developer's name to view their full profile, including detailed statistics and achievements.
</Tip>

## How Time Is Calculated

Your leaderboard time is the sum of all coding activity tracked by the CoreDump editor plugin:

1. **Active coding only** - Idle time doesn't count
2. **All languages combined** - Every language contributes to your total
3. **Automatic tracking** - No manual entry required
4. **Real-time updates** - Your rank changes as you code

<Note>
Leaderboard positions update continuously. Code more to climb higher, but watch out - other developers are coding too!
</Note>

## Reset Schedule

Understanding when leaderboards reset helps you strategize:

**24-Hour Leaderboard**
- Resets: Daily at midnight UTC
- Fresh rankings every day
- Great for daily coding challenges

**7-Day Leaderboard**
- Resets: Every Monday at midnight UTC
- Shows weekly consistency
- Rewards sustained effort over several days

### Countdown Timer

The leaderboard page displays a countdown showing time remaining until the next reset. Use this to:
- Push for a final coding session before reset
- Know when your ranking locks in
- Plan your coding schedule strategically

<Info>
All reset times use UTC timezone. Convert to your local timezone to know exactly when resets occur in your area.
</Info>

## Climbing the Leaderboard

To improve your ranking:

### 1. Code Consistently
Daily coding activity keeps you competitive on both leaderboards. Even short sessions add up.

### 2. Participate in Both Timeframes
Focus on the 24-hour board for quick wins, and the 7-day board for sustained achievement.

### 3. Track Peak Hours
Notice when top developers are most active and adjust your schedule if you're competing seriously.

### 4. Maintain Your Streak
While streaks don't directly affect leaderboard position, they encourage the daily consistency that does.

<Tip>
Set a daily coding goal based on the average time of the top 10 developers. This gives you a realistic target to aim for.
</Tip>

## Leaderboard Statistics

The leaderboard header shows:
- **Time until reset** - Countdown to next ranking refresh
- **Total participants** - Number of developers on the leaderboard
- **Current leader** - Profile preview of the #1 ranked developer

## Viewing the Leaderboard

Access the leaderboard from:
- The main navigation menu
- Direct URL: `/leaderboard`
- Links from developer profiles

<Note>
You must have logged at least one coding session to appear on the leaderboard. New users appear after their first tracked activity.
</Note>

## Fair Competition

CoreDump leaderboards are designed for fair competition:

- **Only active coding counts** - No artificial inflation
- **Language-agnostic** - All programming languages have equal weight
- **Transparent metrics** - Everyone sees the same data
- **Regular resets** - Multiple chances to reach the top

## Leaderboard Strategy

Some tips for competitive developers:

**For 24-Hour Board:**
- Schedule longer coding sessions on days when you can focus
- Code early in the UTC day to maximize your window
- Monitor your position throughout the day

**For 7-Day Board:**
- Consistency beats sporadic long sessions
- Don't skip days - every day counts
- Balance quality coding with quantity of time

<Tip>
The 7-day leaderboard often rewards consistency over intensity. Coding 2 hours daily beats a single 10-hour weekend session.
</Tip>

## What Leaderboards Don't Show

To maintain privacy and focus on coding activity:

- **Code content** - Only time and language are tracked
- **Project details** - What you're building stays private
- **Exact timestamps** - When you code isn't displayed
- **Activity gaps** - Only active coding time shows

<Info>
Leaderboards are optional. If you prefer private tracking, simply use your dashboard without checking rankings.
</Info>
